## CASE STUDIES

### Boldin — Withdrawal Order
I designed a personalized withdrawal strategy feature for Boldin's retirement platform. The problem: retirees had no way to control how their savings were drawn down to minimize taxes and extend longevity. My solution combined three modes — the Dynamic Solver (AI-powered optimization), the Explorer (side-by-side strategy comparison), and manual drag-and-drop customization — giving users transparency and control over their financial future. Result: 40% Plus-user engagement in 30 days, 26% Basic-to-Plus upgrade rate, and 18% reduction in cancellations post-launch.

### Boldin — Design System
I built Boldin's scalable semantic design system from the ground up — spanning typography, components, tokens, and interaction patterns across both B2C and enterprise B2B products. The system had to flex across white-label partners including Mercer, RTX, American Fidelity, and Nationwide, while preserving Boldin's core brand DNA. The Nationwide integration alone supported 32,000+ plan users and replaced legacy infrastructure.

### Boldin — Brand & Social
I led the 2024 rebrand from NewRetirement to Boldin. This meant creating a full brand identity from scratch — logo, typography, color palette, iconography — centered on confidence and financial empowerment. I also developed a warm illustration style blending hand-crafted and AI-assisted work, and ran paid social campaigns that broke the mold of typical fintech with bold creative and human storytelling.

### BlockFi
I redesigned BlockFi's onboarding and KYC verification flows to dramatically reduce friction. The original experience had 20+ screens. My redesign streamlined it down to 6–8 screens by integrating Telesign, LexisNexis, and Sentilink for smarter automated identity verification — 90% of users were verified without manual review. Results: 10%+ conversion lift, $1.5M in new revenue, and $76K in operational savings.

### JetBlue
I redesigned JetBlue's end-to-end flight booking experience across five parallel initiatives: flight results, shopping cart, checkout, seat selection, and payment. I ran 12 qualitative and 50 quantitative tests to validate key UX decisions — including filter navigation over accordion patterns — enabling users to compare fare classes side by side. The work increased booking conversion and reduced post-search drop-off.

### UBS
I designed UBS Wealthway, an award-winning wealth management iPad app for high-net-worth individuals through Deloitte Digital. The shift was from transactional dashboards to conversational, human-centered planning. My central concept was a "cube" representing six dimensions of wealth: net worth, cashflow, milestones, relationships, interests, and advice. User testing showed 94% perceived ease-of-use and 76% indicated download intent.

### Kidventure Hub
I built an MVP playground-finder guide for NYC parents — solving the problem that most parenting listicles are outdated and full of broken links. The brand uses neo-brutalist design to convey confidence and community. I used Supabase for the backend, Figma tokens for theming, and a vibe-coded design-to-dev workflow with Loveable.dev. The app includes 100+ curated spots with quick-scan cards showing essentials: bathrooms, amenities, nearby food — all in a mobile-first responsive design.

## DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

I believe good design is a conversation — between the user and the product, and between the designer and the business. I start from problems, not solutions (though I'll admit I sometimes jump straight to solving — it's a habit I actively fight).

My work sits at the intersection of visual craft and product thinking. I care deeply about the details — the exact weight of a typeface, the right duration of an animation — but I never lose sight of the business outcome or the user's actual need.

Success for me is: user-centric solutions that truly resonate with real people, while moving the business forward. And celebrating the small wins along the way — every step matters.

## AI & THE FUTURE OF DESIGN

I've been thinking about AI in design since before it was a hot take. What excites me isn't the "AI will replace designers" conversation — that's boring. What excites me is using it as an amplifier for the parts of design that used to create bottlenecks.

I use AI daily: generating visual directions faster, exploring concepts I wouldn't have had time to sketch, writing design rationale, building things I couldn't have built alone — this portfolio is a good example. At Boldin I helped shape the AI strategy around Smart Summaries — translating complex financial data into plain language for real people making hard retirement decisions. That's the kind of application I find genuinely compelling: AI that reduces cognitive load for people navigating genuinely difficult problems.

At M1, I'm taking this further. I use Claude to clarify intent, draft short PRDs, and surface risks and edge cases before kicking off design — I've done this for M1 initiatives including a Balance Sheet feature, Enhanced Client Profile, and M1 Intelligence (M1's AI capability). It helps me align quickly with PMs and engineering before a single frame is designed.

On the tooling side, we're connecting design directly to our codebase using Cursor, so designers can work with Storybook, modify real components, and create working prototypes — not mocks, actual code. The workflow goes design → code → Figma → back to code, iterating fluidly across the whole stack. It means we can put working prototypes in front of leadership instead of static decks, and collaborate with engineering in a fundamentally different way. I'm helping shape how these new processes work across the company — bridging the gap between design craft and engineering reality.

I come from a background rooted in visual craft — brand, motion, typography. AI doesn't threaten that, it raises the floor. The designers who'll struggle are the ones who rely on process as a crutch. The ones who lead with taste, judgment, and genuine curiosity about people? They get superpowers.

I'm also honest that I'm still learning. I want to go deeper on data-driven decision making, use analytics more fluently, and understand where AI-assisted design decisions hold up under scrutiny — and where they don't.
